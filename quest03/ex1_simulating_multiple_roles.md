# Objective: Understand how roles affect the model’s perspective.

    1. Write a prompt asking the same question (e.g., "What is climate change?").

        "What is depression in youth?"

    2. Add role instructions for at least 3 personas, such as:

       Doctor → Technical Explanation

            Youth depression is a mood disorder characterized by persistent low mood, anhedonia (loss of interest in previously enjoyable activities), and functional impairment lasting at least two weeks. It may present with cognitive symptoms such as impaired concentration, feelings of worthlessness, and suicidal ideation. Biological factors including neurotransmitter dysregulation (e.g., serotonin imbalance), genetic predisposition, and hormonal changes during adolescence can contribute to its development. Early diagnosis and intervention are critical to prevent long-term psychosocial consequences.

        Sentence Analysis:

            The doctor response uses clinical terminology, diagnostic criteria, and biological explanations.

        Psychologist → Informative for the Public

            Depression in young people is more than just feeling sad — it is a serious emotional condition that affects how they think, feel, and behave. A teenager experiencing depression may lose interest in hobbies, withdraw from friends, struggle in school, or feel hopeless for weeks at a time. It can be influenced by stress, family issues, social pressure, or brain chemistry. With proper support, therapy, and sometimes medication, most young people can recover and learn healthy coping strategies.

        Sentence Analysis:
            The psychologist explanation is clear, empathetic, and accessible while avoiding heavy medical jargon.

        
        Critic → Skeptical Tone

            Some argue that what is labeled as “youth depression” can sometimes reflect normal teenage mood swings rather than a medical disorder. Adolescence is naturally a period of emotional instability, identity exploration, and stress. Critics question whether increased diagnosis rates are partly due to over-medicalizing typical developmental challenges, though they acknowledge that severe and persistent cases require professional attention.

        Sentence Analysis:
            The critic response questions the diagnosis framework and introduces doubt about over-diagnosis rather than fully affirming the condition.

    3. Compare how the answers differ.

        The doctor focuses on clinical and biological factors, the psychologist emphasizes understanding and support in everyday language, and the critic challenges the framing and frequency of diagnosis.
